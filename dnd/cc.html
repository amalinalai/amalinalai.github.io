Creating (The Skeleton of) Your Character

There's no fixed way to create your character! You may start with a character concept & background first and then try to fit that into the game mechanics. However, the following approach is generally how I do it and what I think is the easiest way for new players to begin crafting a character. This should provide a good starting point and will allow us to speed up the finalization of your character during our first session.

Do note that I have simplified the character creation for this campaign to be more welcoming to new players, so if you are an experienced player you may notice some things missing.

Step 1: Choose a Class

Your character's class determines a large portion of your character's abilities. For example, certain classes will allow you to cast spells, while others will allow you to be better at melee rather than ranged combat. Within each class, there are also subclasses. Certain classes will require you to pick a subclass at first level, while others will come later.

All of you will start at level 1, and as you play, your character will level up and unlock more skills and abilities. For this campaign, the following are the allowed classes. You may read through them and pick one for your character.

Barbarian

The defining feature of a barbarian is its presence. The mere presence of a barbarian in a fight can greatly swing the outcome. Barbarians only ever have 1 real ability, their rage. Most of their abilities and perks merely add on to their rage, making them more fearsome while raging. Thus, barbarians are always a force to be reckoned with on the battlefield.

Main ability score: Strength
Secondary ability score: Constitution
Conventional roles filled: DPS, Tank, Crowd Control
Hit points (health) at level 1: 12 + your constitution modifier

Level Ability Description
1st Rage, Unarmored Defense Rage is the most essential piece of the barbarian’s kit, giving them increased survivability and damage for a period of time Unarmored Defense gives the barbarian a bonus to Armor Class while not wearing any armor
2nd Reckless Attack, Danger Sense Reckless Attack grants the barbarian advantage on an attack, but also gives attacks against the barbarian advantage Danger Sense gives the barbarian advantage on Dexterity saving throws
3rd Primal Path These are the barbarian’s subclass options: Path of Berserker and Path of the Totem Warrior
Build Showcase #1: Berserker

Beast is a 5th level berserker wearing a chain shirt and wielding a greataxe. Standing at a cliff edge and seeing the battle below, Beast decides to leap straight off the cliff to land in the fray. Typically, a fall from 50 feet would result in 5d10 bludgeoning damage, however Beast activates his Frenzy as he falls, instead receiving only half damage from the fall. Now in the midst of battle, he can attack up to 3 times per turn, each attack dealing 1d12 slashing damage. As he beats back wave after wave of foot soldiers, Beast eventually notices a fireball spell being flung towards him. His Danger Sense grants him advantage on the Dexterity saving throw to half the incoming damage, so he manages to escape relatively unscathed. As his rage runs its course, he gains one level of exhaustion, imposing disadvantage on all ability checks. He recognises that the time has come to retreat, and he thus leaves the fight with a trail of blood behind him.

Build Showcase #2: Eagle Totem Warrior

Akela is a 5th level totem warrior, wielding a greatsword and wearing no armor. She has embraced the eagle as her spirit animal and is currently tracking down bandits in the forest with her party. As they try to discern the bandits’ whereabouts, Akela sits down and prepares a simple ritual: speak with animals. She asks the birds and the deer whether they had seen bandits in these woods, and they point her in the right direction. As they near the bandit camp, they are spotted by a lookout. Quickly, Akela enters her Rage and uses the Eagle’s ability to Dash as a bonus action, granting her a whopping 80 ft of movement for the turn, allowing her to immediately catch the lookout. Akela then proceeds to use her action to make a Reckless Attack, believing that successfully connecting her attack would finish the bandit off. She makes her first attack with advantage and her second attack with no advantage, finishing off the bandit before he could make sue of the advantage he would have had on attacks against Akela. With this, Akela calls the rest of her party over and they proceed into the bandit camp with caution.

Cleric

Clerics in traditional RPGs are usually healers and supports, but in DnD Clerics can fill a wide variety of roles, depending on the god they draw their power from. Using divine magic, clerics have one of the most diverse subclasses, second only to wizards. Clerics are also defined by their deity, since different deities correspond to different divine domains.

Main ability score: Wisdom
Secondary ability score: Strength/Constitution
Conventional roles filled: DPS, Tank, Healer, Crowd Control, Utility
Hit points (health) at level 1:8 + your constitution modifier

Level Ability Description
1st Spellcasting, Divine Domain Clerics are divine spellcasters, so they make use of a wide variety of spells, with the number of spells available and the number of spell slots available increasing with almost every level

The Divine Domain of a cleric is its subclass, also defining the role of the cleric. Domains also give a cleric access to domain spells that are always available to the cleric
2nd Channel Divinity, Divine Domain Feature Channel Divinity is an ability all clerics have with various effects depending on their domain
- - Gain more spell slots at this level.
DomainsDescription
Life Life clerics are similar to clerics in traditional RPGs, serving a more supportive role in the party. Their domain spells include healing and buff spells that keep their party members healthy and free from status ailments. From 1st level they have access to Disciple of Life, making their healing spells more potent. At 2nd level, they gain access to a new Channel Divinity option: Preserve Life. This ability allows the cleric to perform an emergency mass heal to severely injured allies around them.
Light Light clerics focus on ranged damage dealing, with a focus on fire and radiant damage. Their domain spells take them on the offensive, enabling high damage and good crowd control. At 1st level, they can use a Warding Flare to momentarily blind an enemy, giving them disadvantage on the attack. At 2nd level, they gain the Channel Divinity option: Radiance of the Dawn, dispelling magical darkness and dealing radiant damage to all around them.
Knowledge Knowledge clerics focus on learning and gaining information, as information about the enemy can be your strongest weapon. Their domain spells focus around the mind, being able to influence the minds of others and being able to receive prophetic visions. At 1st level, they receive Blessings of Knowledge, giving them expertise with ability checks related to knowledge. Their new Channel Divinity option at 2nd level is Knowledge of the Ages, granting them proficiency in a skill or tool they would otherwise not have.
Nature Nature clerics use the powers of the natural world such as using animals and plants to protect allies and defeat foes. Their domain spells focus on utility, from gaining information through animal allies, to changing the battlefield’s terrain to their own advantage. At 1st level, as an Acolyte of Nature, nature clerics can learn a druid cantrip to add to their utility. At 2nd level, their Channel Divinity options include Charm Animals and Plants: coercing nearby plants and animals into being charmed by the cleric.
Tempest Tempest clerics channel the power of storms to rebuke their enemies and protect their allies. Their domain spells focus on controlling the battlefield, obscuring allies in fog or pushing enemies away from them. From 1st level storm clerics can invoke the Wrath of the Storm to deal lightning or thunder damage to their attackers. At 2nd level, the storm cleric’s damage output increases further with their Channel Divinity option: Destructive Wrath, causing their lightning or thunder damage to deal maximum damage rather than having to roll.
Trickery Trickery clerics utilise illusory magic and the manipulation of shadows to sow discord into the world. Their domain spells create illusions and enchantments to beguile the minds of their enemies. At 1st level, they can grant the Blessing of the Trickster, allowing them to conceal their allies more effectively. At 2nd level, their Channel Divinity option is to Invoke Duplicity, creating an illusory copy of themselves. This double can cast spells and create distractions, allowing the cleric an advantage in combat.
War War clerics focus on charging into the fray and using brute force to carve a path through their foes. Their domain spells grant them increased survivability as well as bonus damage to their melee strikes. From 1st level, they are War Priests, which grant them additional attacks at the cost of a bonus action. At 2nd level, their Channel Divinity grants them supernatural accuracy for their melee attacks in the form of a Guided Strike.
Build Showcase #1: Light Domain

Ander is a 5th level light cleric who serves under Freyr, the Vanir god of the sun, wearing a chain shirt and wielding a simple mace and shield. As his allies get surrounded by a horde of zombies, Ander raises his shield bearing the symbol of his deity and speaks a holy prayer, using his Channel Divinity to Turn Undead. Most of the zombies are turned to dust by the divine energy, while the few that remain flee from Ander’s shield. As his allies chase down and finish off the zombies, Ander's attention turns to the zombie warlock that resisted the turning effect. Before the warlock can launch its eldritch blast spell at one of his allies, Ander uses his reaction to create a Warding Flare, causing the warlock to miss its attack. Calling for his allies to stand back, Ander finally casts a fireball spell to deal 8d6 fire damage and burn the warlock's body, leaving nothing but ashes behind.

Build Showcase #2: Tempest Domain

Ignatius is a 5th level tempest cleric whose deity is Thor, Aesir god of thunder. He wears full chain mail and swings a large warhammer. When faced with overwhelming odds, Ignatius quickly casts fog cloud to obscure the vision of his enemies and allies alike, allowing his party members to beat a hasty retreat. As they turn to run, a lucky swing of a sword lands against Ignatius’ back, dealing minor damage. With his reaction, Ignatius uses the Wrath of the Storm to launch a bolt of lightning back towards his attacker, dealing 2d8 lightning damage. While arrows whiz past their heads, Ignatius’ party draws their pursuers into a narrow corridor. Here, Ignatius whips around and casts thunderwave, pushing all their pursuers back to create some breathing room for his party’s spellcasters. While the spell usually deals 2d8 thunder damage, Ignatius uses his Channel Divinity to invoke his deity’s Destructive Wrath, dealing 16 thunder damage to all enemies instead.
