Lamy Safari (Yellow!), Medium Nib (Black)

Currently inked with: Pilot Iroshizuku — Kiri-same

This Lamy Safari was my first proper investment in a fountain pen ($30 felt like a lot at that time). I remember the excitement of finding it in a bookstore in Geneva during my trip to CERN in June 2019, and I caved & bought it. I can still remember the weight of it in my hand as my heart fluttered with joy at finally owning a proper fountain pen.

It has been my daily pen ever since. It writes well, reliably and feels comfortable to hold, even during long amounts of writing. I've dropped it plenty, and it's still holding up wonderfully.

Inkstand (Kakimori) Aluminium Rollerball Pen

Currently inked with: Custom Inkstand ink, made in Dec 2018 in Tokyo

Although not a fountain pen, it also makes use of bottled ink. Instand inks are highly pigmented and will ruin other normal fountain pens, which I had to learn the hard way. :-( Although I had another rollerball pen from Inkstand which I bought when I made the ink in Tokyo in 2018, I had dropped it and it stopped writing.

Finally, in October 2021, I found out that inkstand has an online store and got this pen!!

I love this pen for casual writing. It has a nice body, if not slightly narrow, but (it) is still comfortable to write with.

TWSBI VAC700R (Iris), Fine Nib

Currently inked with: Dominant Industry Ink — Royal Azalea

This was a graduation gift I had bought for myself (in 2022)! Enamoured by the vacuum piston filling mechanism, and the transparent ink chamber, I decided to splurge and buy my most expensive pen (at that time). The colours of the pen are absolutely gorgeous and is a pen that sits on my desk as I dare not to bring it out of the house.

Although gorgeous, I have a hard time with writing long amounts of text with this pen due to my fingers digging into the cap screw threads while gripping the pen. It's still a very cool pen though, and I have fun refilling the ink when I get to. :-)

Traveler's Company Brass Fountain Pen, Fine Nib

Currently inked with: Monteverde Ink — Black

I was fascinated by the idea of the pen aging (through patina) depending on how you hold the pen. It's been interesting to see how the pen has oxidized more around where I grip the pen, despite it being only about a year old (I bought this in May 2023). I also absolutely adore how compact it is!

I find myself reaching out for this pen quite a bit as it writes so well. It does have a narrow grip, which makes it hard to hold comfortably for extended amounts of writing. Its nib also seems to keep unscrewing slightly for me, but I still really like this pen! I may consider changing out the nib in the future.

Artefuegoventura Glass Dip Pen

Ink used here: Vinta Inks — Liquid Sepia

My most expensive pen to date, I found this pen on Etsy & fell in love with the beautiful colours on its body. it sits right below my computer screen at my desk, and I'm happy to finally be able to support artisans & their craft ♡ Dip pens take a bit of fiddling (with) and I'm not 100% sure I've got a hang of writing with it just yet. I will keep practising! I bought this in Dec 2023 as a small gift for myself.
