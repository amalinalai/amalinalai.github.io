bookbug page

aka in my intellectual era

I chanced upon the Bookbug club, which is a monthly online book club for personal websites. I've always loved reading, but I've never actually been part of a book club before! I did participate in a "book club" with some online friends before, but it only lasted for one book, and I was so nervous about the whole thing. There's something quite daunting about sharing and writing my opinions on books I've read, as I've noted in my bookshelf page, but I wanted to push myself out of my comfort zone. I thought it'd also be fun to not only try to be more critical in my reading, but to also translate those thoughts into a piece of cohesive writing.

version 1.0 › september 2024 — ???

I opted for a really simple design (that somehow still took a lot of time fixing) using flexbox. Honestly, I almost gave up with the flexbox and thought of using CSS grids instead, but I persevered and made it work! I wanted to make each review look a bit like index cards you'd pull out from a filing cabinet, thus the black rounded tabs with the corresponding book month. In a way, because of the chronological and structured nature of book reading in the book club, I thought this was a cute way to design the page. The transform: rotate(); property honestly gave me such a difficult time, but I had it figured out in the end after realizing you could choose the pivot about which you rotated the divs.

The adorable worm was drawn by maple and can be obtained from the bookbug website! I thought it was a lovely illustration that I simply had to use, and the link colours of the page were taken from this illustration. Because the worm reminded me of having a nice picnic and reading under the tree, I chose a green plaid background that's reminiscent of picnic mats.

Because I used flexboxes, the responsive design came along really easily. I just had to unset the rotation transformations on some of the divs and change how some of the elements flex-wrap, but at this point I've had quite a bit of practice with the latter after all the pages I've coded for this website that it was not difficult.
