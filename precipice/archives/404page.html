404 page

aka the fever dream pages

Typically, the 404 page on most websites are rather boring: they tend to display the error code, tell you the page is not found and link back to the main website. Occassionally, they might include a cute image of their mascot holding up some kind of 404 sign. However, in January 2023 I chanced upon 5amgirlfriend's 404 page which chronicles the day of "maid-chan", the purported housemaid of 5amgirlfriend. I adored it, its warmth and how it turned the 404 page from something purely perfunctory to something more. So tickled by this idea, I wanted something more for my 404 page as well.

version 1.0 › mid 2023 — ???

In some ways, the 404 page is a liminal space — a transitory place between the "wrong" or non-existent page, and the "right" page. I wanted to lean heavily into that and underscore its liminality; rather than being a page one typically quickly navigates away from, I wanted to draw out the duration of time one spent in this space. I settled on a (very short) text-based adventure, as those websites I also often associated with liminal spaces with how barebones they often looked. It was also a good opportunity to write a short "story" — something I enjoyed doing.

The short adventure itself involves the concepts of warped time; a space in which time doesn't move and instead is still for as long as you wanted to remain in it. In some ways, I was drawn to the idea of respite, of pausing time as a way to get a breather from everything else. Perhaps it's a reflection of my psyche at the time of writing: a yearning to have a space just like that to settle for a little while, a refuge from the stressors of life. It's called the fever dream pages because of this — reading and writing it makes me feel like I'm in one.

Originally I intended for the short adventure to be something longer, with more winding paths and branches to follow, but I was coding and writing it at a point in time where I didn't feel like I had too much time to spare. I thus settled on the current length, though I am not opposed to extending it in the future.

The layout itself is simple, and I made use of 1-Line Layouts' Clamping My Style template. I also made use of the details element to allow visitors to decide what to do by clicking on the summary, and have the adventure respond to their choices by displaying the details on-click.
