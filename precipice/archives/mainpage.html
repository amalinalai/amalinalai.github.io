main page

aka the professional page(s)

As mentioned, this entire website / domain was originally intended to be for my professional digital identity as I embarked on my PhD. It was early 2023, and I had just come back from a conference where I realised that email exchange was one way of making connections, but having a website allowed others to also find you easily and have your credentials on hand. Delighted at the opportunity to code my own layout and craft my own space, I created a domain and started working on it. The professional side of this domain, which I dub the main page, is actually a collection of a few pages pertaining to my identity as a PhD student, all with similar layouts.

version 1.0 › early 2023

I looked to a lot of other scientists to what information they included in their websites. Obviously, as I had no publications yet, I stuck to a main page with a short introduction, a page for my CV and another for presentations.

At that point in time, I was deeply enamoured by lightweight and brutalist web design, so I went with a simple layout I could easily make responsive as well. I also drew inspiration from many older scientists whose websites look (probably almost exactly) like when they were first made in the early 2000s. The first version had a font size that was marginally too big, an photo I had to Photoshop a friend out of, and less words about myself. I remember the horizontal line on the header was something I drew from 100r.co. I also wasn't sure if adding a link to my personal site (hosted on the same domain) was considered 'professional' enough to do, so I oscillated between including it and not through the few early iterations of it.

The layout itself consists of two floating div elements(the left navigation div and the main body). Within the left navigation, there are two elements, namely the image and the navigation itself. In the desktop site, I didn't do anything special with two inner elements. However, for the responsive site, I added the flex-wrap property to the navigation such that I could:

  1. have the navigation be aligned next to the image, and
  2. use flex-flow: row-reverse; to invert the position of the navigation and the image (i.e. for the navigation to come before the image such that it appears on the left instead of the right of the image).

version 1.0+++ › 2023 — ???

Over time, I slowly refined my page by modifying the content, reducing the font size, adding more links, changing out the main image, etc. I even changed how the links looked and added hover transitions. Overall though, the general layout remained the same. I'm not sure I have any intention of changing it, as I am quite happy with how it looks and works right now. :-)

In 2024, I added a projects page! I also added a history of all my past blurbs, inspired by Laurel Schwulst's old about pages, where she chronicled how her biography changed with the changing months. It is now unfortunately no longer available on her website.
